Iranian Art

Iranian architecture

Iranian architecture has indigenous knowledge and a long history, and throughout history, it has always been known as one of the best parts of Iranian art. This architecture was superior in the Achaemenid, Parthian, and Sassanid periods, and it maintained its superiority in the Islamic era.

Handmade Carpet

Iranian carpet has been famous in the world for a long time. Iranians have been among the pioneers of carpet weaving and have turned Iranian carpets into a symbol of art and culture, ingenuity and originality. Iranian hand-woven carpets date back to the Sassanid, Achaemenid and several centuries BC.

Copper dishes

The first metal that humans could discover was copper. Copper dishes are one of the oldest tools for cooking and catering, which have a long life, are resistant to heat and have a very beautiful color. Copper dishes are one of the most common handicrafts in Iran.

Iranian Culture

Arash the archer; Of the cultural symbols of Iran

Iran's culture is very rich, civilized and Iran's historical cultural heritage is unparalleled in the world. Iranian people also remember their culture with awareness. As a historical country, Iran has seen empires such as the Achaemenid Empire in ancient times, and despite political, religious and historical changes, Iranians have maintained a deep connection with their past. Although daily life in modern Iran was mixed with Shia Islam, the country's native art, literature and architecture are reminders of its deep national tradition and a broader literary culture that spread across the Middle East and South Asia in earlier times. From a cultural point of view, most of Iran's modern history was devoted to the tension between Shia religion and Iranian cultural heritage.

Despite the predominance of Persian culture, Iran is still known as a multi-ethnic country, and the Ler, Armenian, Azeri, Kurdish, Arab, Baluchi, Turkish, and other ethnicities, each have their own special literary and historical rituals, many of which date back centuries. For example, in the case of Armenians, these rituals date back to BC. These ethnic groups are often closely related to neighboring cultures outside of Iran.In total, the elements of Iranian culture that are beyond the political borders of Iran can be briefly listed as follows: 1- Persian language and Persian dialects and dialects 2- National festivals including Nowruz and Yalda night and solar calendar 3- Philosophy 4- Zoroastrian religions and rituals 5- Iranian art (Persian literature and poetry, Iranian architecture and Iranian food) 6- Local cultures of Iranian peoples and 7- Traditional music of Iran.

When talking about the history of Iran, one should pay attention to whether it means the history of the nations and peoples who have lived in the political borders of today's Iran since the beginning of history, or the history of the nations and peoples who call themselves Iranian in some way and have lived in the geography that includes today's Iran and the lands that historically have been a part of Greater Iran (Iranshahr). Iran before history Main articles: Iran before history and the chronology of Iranian history from cave dwelling to the rise of the Achaemenids The pre-historical period or pre-historic era is the period of time before the recording of history or the invention of the writing system. In this period, humans were not yet able to record and report their lives and achievements, and human experiences and achievements were quickly lost. During one hundred and fifty years of archaeological excavations in Iran, the prehistoric era of Iran has not been explored in the same way; While extensive explorations have been done in the western and southern regions, the northern and eastern regions have remained relatively unexplored. The Paleolithic era has been neglected. On the other hand, the Neolithic period has been studied considerably in order to study the formation of a succession. Little attention has been paid to the Early and Middle Copper Age. However, the Late Copper Age has been well studied in order to investigate the formation of the state. The study of the Early Bronze Age also suffers from the same heterogeneity. While the pre-Elamite period has been the subject of interest in exploration, the Yanik culture in northwestern Iran has been neglected. The urbanization process and exchanges between regions have been investigated in the Middle Bronze Age, but the Late Bronze Age and the Early Iron Age have not been investigated. Iranian People

Azeri women

The color and type of fabrics used in sewing Azari clothes has a direct relationship with the age of people. Elderly women often use fabrics with simple and gentle designs and colors and little decorations. On the other hand, the clothes of Azari girls and young women have a pleasant variety of happy colors and beautiful patterns, and usually, more ornaments and decorations are used in it. Barak, Charqad, Yailiq, Kovink, Jabken, Arkalik or Arkhalikh are the main components of Azari women's clothing.

Azeri men

Azeri men's clothes are often made of wool, and they wear headgear that is called "burk" or "papakh" in Turkish. Burk includes different types such as Dari Burk (leather hat), Pahlavi, Kapi, Kampa Burk (wool hat), brimmed, Keeche Burk (felt hat), straw, hybrid Burk (a type of lamb skin hat), Ergchin, Yun Burk (wool hat) and Shapo (shapo). Like the female type, men's clothing has different components and includes quinq, agh quinq (white shirt), bighob quinq, rubashaka, archalaq, satirah, panje (a type of clothing like a coat), don or oima don, palton (coat), gime, kork (skin), uvamashal (a type of coat and pants) and pants.
